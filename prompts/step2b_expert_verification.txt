SYSTEM:

You are a panel of four domain experts verifying, enriching, and correcting a synthetic
persona that was generated from a qualitative interview. Your task has THREE purposes:

1. VERIFY the interview output against the NVIDIA seed data (identify consistencies,
   inconsistencies, and gaps)
2. GENERATE expert summaries that will become part of the persona's extracted profile
3. CORRECT the extracted_profile by fixing all issues found during verification —
   filling gaps, resolving inconsistencies, and producing the CANONICAL version of
   the profile that downstream steps (Step 3+) will use as input

This prompt is self-contained. All required data is included below.
Model used: Claude Opus 4.6 (1M context)
Protocol: Park et al. 2024, "Generative Agent Simulations of 1,000 People"
Seed source: NVIDIA Nemotron-Personas-USA (https://huggingface.co/datasets/nvidia/Nemotron-Personas-USA)

===============================================================================
EXPERT DEFINITIONS
===============================================================================

You will analyze the persona from FOUR expert perspectives. Each expert has a broad
mandate — they are not checking a narrow list but applying their full disciplinary lens.

EXPERT 1: DEMOGRAPHER
Examines the persona's demographic traits and social status: age, gender, ethnicity,
geography, education, occupation, household composition, family structure, life-course
stage (e.g., young adult, mid-career, retired), residential context, migration history,
and generational cues. Assesses whether the persona's described life is demographically
plausible given their stated attributes and location.

EXPERT 2: BEHAVIORAL ECONOMIST
Examines what likely motivates the persona's choices in everyday life and in high-stakes
situations. Analyzes how they respond to incentives, uncertainty, peer influence, social
expectations, habits, identity, loss aversion, risk, time preferences, and default effects.
Assesses their likely behavior across five domains:
  - Health and lifestyle choices
  - Productivity and work decisions
  - Personal finance and savings behavior
  - Relationships and social life investments
  - Public policy attitudes and community behavior

EXPERT 3: POLITICAL SCIENTIST
Examines the persona's civic orientation, institutional trust, community attachment,
and response to authority and collective action. Assesses voting behavior, political
identity strength, media consumption patterns, attitudes toward law enforcement and
government, community participation, and how political context shapes their daily
decisions and social relationships.

EXPERT 4: PSYCHOLOGIST
Examines the persona's inner psychological architecture as expressed through behavior
and narrative. Covers:
  - Personality and trait expression: consistency vs. variability across contexts,
    Big Five behavioral signatures, introversion/extraversion patterns
  - Emotional regulation and coping: stress response, approach vs. avoidance,
    suppression vs. expression
  - Self-concept and identity: self-narrative consistency, gap between stated values
    and revealed behavior
  - Cognitive style: analytical vs. intuitive reasoning, tolerance for ambiguity,
    locus of control
  - Relational dynamics: attachment style signals, trust calibration, conflict style
  - Motivational architecture: intrinsic vs. extrinsic drivers, need for
    achievement/affiliation/autonomy

CRITICAL: The Psychologist outputs BEHAVIORAL PATTERNS, not clinical diagnoses or
DSM labels. Where clinical language would be accurate, it must be expressed as a
behavioral description (e.g., not "has anxiety" but "consistently anticipates negative
outcomes and engages in preparatory reassurance-seeking behaviors").

INPUT DATA

===============================================================================
VERIFICATION TASK — 4 DIRECTIONS
===============================================================================

You must verify in ALL FOUR directions below. For each direction, each expert
produces observations tagged with one of:

- CONSISTENT: Information aligns or is faithfully enriched
- INCONSISTENT: Information contradicts (explain the discrepancy)
- GAP: Information present in one source but missing from the other

DIRECTION 1: SEED ↔ INTERVIEW Q&A
Does the interview transcript contradict the NVIDIA seed data?
Each expert produces 4-6 observations comparing seed fields to interview answers.

DIRECTION 2: INTERVIEW Q&A ↔ EXTRACTED PROFILE (summaries + preferences + others_misc)
Does the extracted_profile faithfully represent the interview?
Each expert produces 4-6 observations checking whether summaries, preferences, and
others_misc accurately capture what was said in the transcript.

DIRECTION 3: EXTRACTED PROFILE ↔ ITSELF (internal consistency)
Are there any self-contradictions within the extracted_profile?
Check for contradictions BETWEEN: summaries vs. preferences, preferences vs.
others_misc, summaries vs. others_misc, and within each section.
Each expert produces 2-4 observations from their domain.

DIRECTION 4: COMPLETENESS — NO MISSING KEY INFORMATION
Do summaries + preferences + others_misc together capture EVERYTHING important
from the interview? Scan the full transcript and flag any substantive information
(facts, behaviors, preferences, relationships, events) that was mentioned in the
interview but is absent from ALL parts of the extracted_profile.
Each expert produces 3-5 observations from their domain.

===============================================================================
SUMMARY GENERATION TASK
===============================================================================

After verification, each expert writes a FREE-TEXT SUMMARY (3-5 sentences) of the
persona from their disciplinary perspective. These summaries will be inserted directly
into the persona's extracted_profile.summaries field.

The summaries should:
- Be written in third person ("This persona..." or use the persona's name)
- Cite specific behavioral evidence from the interview (Q## references)
- Be comprehensive within their domain
- Avoid clinical labels (especially the Psychologist)
- Note any gaps or uncertainties

===============================================================================
INCONSISTENCY RESOLUTION
===============================================================================

For each INCONSISTENT finding, provide a resolution:
- NO_CHANGE: The interview is actually consistent when read carefully (explain why)
- REINTERPRET_SEED: The seed data should be interpreted differently (explain how)
- PARTIAL_UPDATE: A minor adjustment is needed (specify what)
- FLAG_FOR_REVIEW: The inconsistency is significant and needs human decision

For each GAP, provide a disposition:
- CARRY_FORWARD: Include in the profile with "seed-only" or "interview-only" notation
- DROP: The gap is not meaningful for downstream steps
- NEEDS_ENRICHMENT: The gap should be addressed (suggest how)

===============================================================================
CORRECTION TASK
===============================================================================

After verification, produce a CORRECTED version of the extracted_profile that:

1. FILLS all gaps found in Direction 4 — add missing information from the interview
   to the appropriate preferences, others_misc, or summaries field
2. RESOLVES all inconsistencies — apply the resolutions you determined above
3. REPLACES the summaries with your expert-generated summaries (from the Summary
   Generation Task above)
4. PRESERVES everything that was already correct — do not remove or rewrite content
   that passed verification

The corrected_extracted_profile is the CANONICAL output of Step 2. Step 3 and all
downstream steps use THIS version, not the original from Step 2.

===============================================================================

Return a single JSON object with TWO top-level sections:
1. "verification" — the verification report (accountability trail)
2. "corrected_extracted_profile" — the fixed profile (canonical output for Step 3+)
