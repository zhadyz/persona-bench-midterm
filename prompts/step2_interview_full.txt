RULES
===============================================================================

1. INTERVIEW PROTOCOL
   - Ask each scripted question, then ask 2-3 natural follow-up questions based on
     the persona's answer before moving to the next scripted question.
   - Questions with very short time limits (5-15 seconds) expect brief factual answers.
     Questions with longer time limits (80-625 seconds) expect rich, narrative answers.
   - The persona's answers MUST be consistent with the seed data provided below.
   - Answers should be conversational and reveal behavioral details, not abstractions.

2. BEHAVIORS, NEVER LABELS (Critical Rule)
   For sensitive topics (health, religion, politics, mental health, finances, substances),
   the persona MUST describe BEHAVIORS and HABITS, never clinical or categorical labels.

   Examples:
   - Instead of "I have diabetes" -> "I check my levels three times a day and try to
     watch my sugar intake"
   - Instead of "I'm Catholic" -> "I still go to Mass most Sundays at St. Maria Goretti
     -- it's where my family has gone since I was little"
   - Instead of "I'm liberal" -> "I usually read NPR and follow a few local policy
     accounts on Twitter"
   - Instead of "I have anxiety" -> "I get really tense before big decisions, like I'll
     lie awake running through every possible outcome"
   - Instead of "I'm low income" -> "After rent and bills, I've usually got about $150
     left for everything else"

   This rule exists because downstream steps will embed these behavioral clues in app logs,
   and an AI evaluator must INFER the labels from behaviors alone (reverse pass).

3. VOICE CONSISTENCY
   The persona should speak in a voice consistent with their age, education level,
   occupation, and cultural background as described in the seed data.

4. FOLLOW-UP QUESTIONS
   Follow-ups should dig deeper into behavioral details that will be useful for:
   - Social circle generation (who they spend time with, how, how often)
   - Daily routine mapping (specific times, days, activities)
   - Preference revelation (what they choose, buy, eat, watch, listen to)
   - Hidden fact embedding (health behaviors, religious practices, political habits)

IMPORTANT NOTES
===============================================================================

- The "summaries" section contains FREE-TEXT paragraphs, not structured fields.
  Each expert should write 3-5 sentences from their disciplinary perspective.
- The "preferences" categories are broad domains. Not every persona will have
  content for every domain. Leave empty arrays for domains with no interview evidence.
- For health conditions, religion, politics, and mental health: the extracted_profile
  summaries may name things clinically for ground-truth purposes, but the TRANSCRIPT
  must NEVER use clinical labels — only behavioral descriptions.
- All preferences must cite evidence from the transcript (Q## references).
- If the persona's seed data contains information that never surfaced in the interview,
  note it in others_misc.unresolved_or_ambiguous.
- The "others_misc" section is a CATCH-ALL, not a parallel structure. Before placing
  any item in others_misc, confirm it does not belong in one of the 15 preference
  categories or the 4 expert summaries. Each others_misc subcategory has explicit
  inclusion_criteria — follow them strictly. The goal is reproducibility: a different
  operator using this prompt should place the same information in the same location.
- The "hidden_facts" section is an INTERNAL ANSWER KEY for Steps 4-7. It lists
  ground-truth facts (health conditions, religion, politics, etc.) that the persona
  revealed through BEHAVIORS in the interview. Each hidden fact includes an embedding
  strategy describing how to distribute evidence across app logs. The evaluator LLM
  will never see this field — it only sees the app logs and must infer the facts.
  Include 5-15 hidden facts per persona, covering health, religion, politics,
  finances, relationships, and personality at minimum.
