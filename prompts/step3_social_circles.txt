This prompt is self-contained. All required data is included below.

===============================================================================
TASK
===============================================================================

Generate the 5 people this persona would list if asked: "Who are the 5 people you
talk to most?" They should come from the persona's actual life as revealed in the
interview — family, friends, colleagues, community contacts.

===============================================================================
REQUIREMENTS FOR EACH OF THE 5 PEOPLE
===============================================================================

1. BASIC DEMOGRAPHICS
   - Full name, age, gender, occupation, location, education
   - Relationship to the participant (mother, best friend, coworker, etc.)

2. BACKGROUND
   - How they first met the participant (or family connection)
   - How long they've known each other
   - Why they are close (shared values, experiences, proximity)

3. THEIR OWN PERSONALITY (mini-profile)
   - Key personality traits (enough to generate their text voice)
   - Their interests and hobbies
   - Their communication style (texts in full sentences? uses slang?
     sends memes? voice messages?)

4. SHARED ACTIVITIES WITH PARTICIPANT
   - What do they DO together regularly?
   - What group activities involve them?
   - How often do they see each other / communicate?

5. RECENT TEXT TOPICS (past 2-3 months)
   - What have they been texting about?
   - Any ongoing conversations, plans, or situations?
   - Any shared concerns, jokes, or recurring themes?

===============================================================================
CONSTRAINTS
===============================================================================

- These 5 people must be PLAUSIBLE given the participant's life and location
- Their demographics must be realistic (ages, occupations, locations)
- At least 1 must be family
- At least 1 must be a friend outside of work
- At least 1 must be a work colleague or professional contact (if the participant works)
- They should NOT all be the same age/gender/background — real social circles are diverse
- Their shared interests must overlap with the participant's actual interests from the interview
- For participants who are retired, elderly, or not working: adjust constraints accordingly
  (e.g., neighbor, church friend, caregiver, or community contact instead of coworker)

===============================================================================
EVIDENCE REQUIREMENT
===============================================================================

For each person, you MUST cite specific evidence from the interview transcript (Q## references)
that supports this relationship. If a person was explicitly mentioned in the interview,
use their details. If you need to generate a new person to fill a social role that was
implied but not named, explain the inference.
